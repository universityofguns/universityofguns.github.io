## Buyer's Guide


### Important Features to Consider

When shopping for velcro gun holsters, there are several features to consider: 

* Retention system: Ensure that the holster has a secure retention system. This can be in the form of a strap, click mechanism, or an automatic lock.

* Comfort: The holster should feel comfortable when worn on your body. Look for a padded back or adjustable belt loops for a customized fit.

* Durability: The material used should be strong and resistant to wear and tear. Velcro is a good choice as it offers a strong grip but can also be easily removed when needed.


### Types of Velcro Gun Holsters

There are various types of velcro gun holsters designed for different purposes: 

* IWB (Inside waistband) holsters: These are designed to be worn inside your waistband, allowing for easy concealment.

* OWB (Outside waistband) holsters: These are typically worn on the outside of your waistband and are perfect for open carry or training.

* Shoulder holsters: Ideal for people who prefer a cross-draw style of carrying their gun.

* Ankle holsters: Good for those looking for a discreet carry option where the gun can be easily accessed if needed.


### Choosing the Right Size and Fit

It's crucial to find a velcro gun holster that fits your specific firearm. Here's how: 

1. Determine the make and model of your gun.

2. Research whether the holster is compatible with your particular firearm or if it's adjustable for various gun sizes.

3. Check the sizing chart provided by the manufacturer to ensure a proper fit.


### Consider Compatibility with Your Wardrobe

When choosing a velcro gun holster, consider how it will work with your daily attire: 

* For IWB holsters, ensure that your pants have enough room to accommodate the holster and gun without causing discomfort or bulk.

* OWB holsters may require a slightly looser belt or larger belt loops to accommodate the holster's attachment points.

* Shoulder holsters should be considered if you wear jackets, blazers, or other garments with a suitable inner lining or pockets to conceal the holster effectively.


### Concealed Carry Permit and Legal Considerations

Before purchasing a velcro gun holster, familiarize yourself with your local laws and regulations regarding concealed carry: 

* Ensure that the holster you choose complies with any requirements set forth by your state or local jurisdiction.

* Consider taking a concealed carry class to learn how to properly and safely carry your firearm with a velcro holster.
