## Buyer's Guide


### Introduction to MOA Targets

<div><a href="https://serp.ly/@universityofguns/amazon/moa-targets?utm_source=universityofguns&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gh-pages"><img src="https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/MOA+Targets-2/w=720,h=540,fit=pad,background=black" alt="MOA Targets-2"></a></div>

MOA targets, also known as Minute of Angle targets, are tools intended for sharpening your rifle's shooting accuracy. Their high precision makes them an indispensable asset for beginners looking to improve their skills, as well as seasoned shooters wanting to maintain their prowess. 

These targets consist of concentric circles that are used to measure the accuracy of firearms. The smallest circle—or the bull's eye—is the target's point of aim, while the larger circles denote degrees of shooting error. An MOA of 1 inch at 100 yards, for instance, signifies that when aiming at the bull's eye, a bullet should ideally strike within this radius at 100 yards distance. 


### Important Features to Consider

* **Accuracy:**  Look for an MOA target with tightly-spaced concentric circles, as this indicates its precision.

* **Durability:**  Choose a target made from high-quality materials that can withstand consistent shooting practice and harsh weather conditions.

* **Portability:**  Select a compact and lightweight design that can be easily carried to and from the shooting range.

* **Range:**  Assess whether the target suits your intended shooting distance. For instance, a 100-yard target is perfect for rifle training, while a smaller 25-yard target is more suitable for pistols.


### General Advice for Using MOA Targets

When using MOA targets, remember the following guidelines: 

1. Proper sighting is crucial to achieving accurate shots. Ensure your rifle is precisely sighted, preferably using a bore sight.

2. Before firing, take into account any environmental factors, such as wind direction and speed, that may impact your aim.

3. When interpreting your results, account for external factors affecting accuracy, like barrel heating or scope mirage. This helps ensure an accurate evaluation of your rifle's performance.

By understanding these features and using them effectively, you can enhance your rifle shooting capabilities and enjoy a more productive time at the shooting range.
